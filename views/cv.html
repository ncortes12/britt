Human-based Research
  • Designing and executing questionnaires within Qualtrics Software Program
  • Independently writing successful IRB protocols, amendments, and renewals
  • Designing novel questionnaires and daily diary prompts
  • Collecting and analyzing data via previously validated questionnaires
    • Includes the NICHD Motor and Social Development Scales, the Early Childhood Behavior Questionnaire, the Attachment Q-Set, the Maternal Postnatal Attachment Questionnaire, the CESDR-Revised Depression Scale, the Parenting Stress Index-4 Short Form, and the Sensory Profile 2
  • Collecting data from young infants via validated interactive tasks
    • Includes the Early Social and Communication Scales (Mundy et al., 2003) and Pearson Clinical Bayley-III Screening Test: Cognitive, Receptive Language, and Expressive Language sub-tests
  • Designing interview prompts and analyzing data from one-on-one participant interviews
  • Training and mentoring undergraduate research assistants in analysis of quantitative and qualitative data collected from humans
Nonhuman-based Research
  • Collecting behavioral and acoustic data from wild and captive lemurs
    • Completed a summer field season for my Master’s Thesis research collecting data from several lemur species at the Duke Lemur Center, Durham, NC, 2012
    • Completed a summer field season collecting my own loud call data from Varecia variegata and acting as a research assistant for James Herrera, PhD on a census project at Ranomafana National Park, Madagascar, 2011
  • Training and mentoring undergraduate research assistants in collection and analysis of behavioral data collected from nonhuman primates
  • Natural sound recording using digital recording equipment
    • Completed the Natural Sound Recording Workshop at the San Francisco State Field Campus through Cornell University Bioacoustics Research Program, 2013
  • Bioacoustic analysis using Raven and XBAT Software programs
    • Completed the Sound Analysis Workshop (SAW) at the Cornell Lab of Ornithology through Cornell University Bioacoustics Research Program, 2012
    • Completed analysis of infrasonic components in mountain gorilla close contact calls in collaboration with Drs. N. & H. D. Steklis at the University of Arizona.
